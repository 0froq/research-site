## Thoughts

Let’s figure all the things out.

Mean and trend can be calculated from the temperature time series. Mean means the average temperature level, and trend means the warming speed.

If we do a first-difference on it, we can get the overall acceleration by the trend of the 1st-difference series. E.g., the acceleration is i °C/year², meaning that the warming speed increases i °C/year every year on average.

And, since we have the great STARS algorithm, we can also detect the regime shifts in the 1st-difference (hereinafter referred to as 1d) series. STARS detects the shifts in the mean of the series. Regimes differ in the mean. So if we apply STARS to the 1d series, we can detect the shifts in the warming speed, with each shifts representing a change in the warming speed.
