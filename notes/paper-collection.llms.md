# Hiatus Paper Collection

Collected sentences and excerpts from related papers, useful for writing the warming acceleration paper.

## Winslow et al. (2018) — ERL

- 155 lakes globally; 83% exhibited reduced warming rates during the hiatus
- Two-period comparison (pre-hiatus vs hiatus) rather than changepoint detection
- Lake-specific response heterogeneity not explained by morphometric attributes

## Zhou et al. (2023) — Nature Communications

- *“the rate of lake heating slows as air warms”*
- Evaporative cooling as negative feedback on lake warming
- Local climate (air temp, wind, humidity, irradiance), land cover, geomorphology, and water transparency explain \>30% of lake–air temperature difference variance
- Clear, cold, deep, high-altitude, undisturbed lakes are most sensitive to air temperature changes

## O’Reilly et al. (2015) — GRL

- Global lake summer SST warming at ~0.34 °C/decade
- GLAST dataset: satellite-derived lake surface water temperature

## Woolway et al. (2017) — Climatic Change

- Wind stilling → extended stratification → surface warming amplification
- UK lake simulations

## Magnuson et al. (2000) — Science

- Systematic documentation of long-term ice season shortening in N. hemisphere lakes

## Medhaug et al. (2017) — Nature

- Multi-mechanism review of the global warming hiatus
- Ocean heat uptake, internal variability, incomplete forcings

## Lund et al. (2023) — J. Climate

- Best practices for climate changepoint detection
- Sensitivity to assumptions; external validation needed for attribution
